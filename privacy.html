Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

myShakeEat (“the app”) is a tool that helps you decide where to eat: shake your phone and it picks a restaurant near you at random. The app has no accounts and no server of its own, and does not collect personal information such as your name or email. This policy explains what data the app uses and why.

Location

To find restaurants near you, the app requests your approximate location when you shake. Your coordinates are sent to the following services to look up nearby restaurants:

  • Google Places API — to find nearby restaurants and their details (name, rating, price level, opening hours, phone).
  • OpenStreetMap / Overpass API — for supplementary public map data such as halal markers.

The app does not store your location on any server and does not use it for tracking. Location is only used when you actively shake to search. You can turn off location permission in your system settings at any time.

Advertising

The app uses Google AdMob to show ads. AdMob may collect your device's advertising identifier and usage data to serve and measure ads. This processing is governed by the Google Privacy Policy. You can reset or limit your advertising identifier in your system settings.

Local storage (on your device only)

The following data is stored only on your phone (the app's local storage) and is never uploaded anywhere:

  • History — restaurants you confirmed with “Let's go with it”, and the date.
  • Preferences — search radius, theme, language.
  • Recent picks — used to avoid recommending the same place twice in a row.

Uninstalling the app, or clearing history inside the app, deletes this data. See Data deletion.

Maps

The map thumbnail on the result page comes from OpenStreetMap; “Take me there” opens navigation in the maps app on your device (such as Google Maps).

Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

If we update this policy, we will revise this page and update the “Last updated” date at the top.
